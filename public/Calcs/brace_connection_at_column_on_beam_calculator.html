③ Beam A992 Fy=50 ksi

Collector Pc override — what it is and where to find it. The §H1 interaction row treats the brace horizontal component as a collector force in the bare beam (Pr = ΣHT). AISC 360-22 §H1.1 defines Pc as the available axial compressive strength per Chapter E — i.e., including member buckling, not just yield. Because the collector's effective unbraced length is a global property of your model, this calc does not invent a KL/r (logged deviation D-3): with the field at 0 it uses φFyAg (the yield ceiling) and the H1 row reports REVIEW, never a plain PASS. Source for the override value: RISA-3D member Detail Report → "AISC 360-22 LRFD Code Check" table → "Axial Compression Analysis — Available" (that is RISA's φcPn per Chapter E with your Lb/K settings), or a hand Chapter E calc. Enter it here to convert the H1 row from REVIEW to a design-value check.

Can Lc,avg be computed? Yes. For this topology the gusset's only supported boundary is the beam flange, so the DG29 three-line construction reduces to straight lines from the Whitmore section to the flange, measured parallel to the brace axis: L2 = st at the brace centerline and L1/L3 = st ∓ (Lw/2)·cotθ at the Whitmore ends (clipped at 0 where the fan meets the flange), with Lw = wb + 2·lw·tan30°. With "Auto-compute" checked the field is filled from st, θ, wb, lw and the three lines are printed beside the checkbox and drawn in the gusset sketch below the results. Uncheck to enter a measured value from the detail drawing instead.

⑩ Transfer Force to Beam-End Connections Dowswell

What this is. The transfer force is the axial force that must pass THROUGH the beam and its end connections to keep the brace's horizontal component in equilibrium with the rest of the lateral system (Dowswell, Transfer Forces in Steel Structures, AISC). It is a function of the load path, not of any one member's force diagram, and it is NOT generally equal to the beam axial force your analysis prints.

Rigid-diaphragm caveat — read before trusting a zero. If your RISA model uses a rigid diaphragm, the diaphragm constraint absorbs in-plane axial force at the node instantly, so the beam's axial-force diagram reads ≈0 even though the brace is dumping ΣHT kips into the steel at this gusset. That zero is an artifact of the idealization: in reality the horizontal component enters the BEAM at the gusset interface and can only leave through the headed studs into the slab over a finite transfer length. Recommended treatment: (1) this calc already takes the §H1 collector demand as Pr = ΣHT in the bare steel, independent of the analysis axial — that is deliberate and stays; (2) enter the transfer force here from a Dowswell-style free-body of the node (a defensible default when the diaphragm takes everything locally is ΣHT delivered over the stud group each side, with the beam-end connections seeing whatever your free-body says continues past them); (3) check the stud group for the ADDED shear demand: nstuds ≥ ΣHT/Qn over the chosen transfer length, on top of the composite-flexure demand already accounted in Calc 3 — studs inside the blockout do not exist, so the transfer starts outside it. Delivery to the far-end connections remains a REVIEW gate — this calc does not design them.
