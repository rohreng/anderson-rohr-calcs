Engineering Notes — Belled Pier Design:
  • No skin friction: Belled piers are designed for end bearing only. Skin friction is ignored because (a) negative skin friction may act downward in compressible soils and (b) the bell provides all required capacity through bearing.
  • Bell capacity: Qall = Abell × qend where qend is the allowable bearing pressure from the geotechnical report at the bell elevation.
  • Bell diameter ratio: Bell diameter is typically 2× to 3× the shaft diameter. Ratios above 3:1 may be difficult to construct and require special equipment. Minimum practical bell diameter depends on drilling equipment.
  • Bell angle: A 45° bell angle (1:1 slope from horizontal) is the standard minimum. Steeper angles require more precise drilling. Some jurisdictions require a minimum 45° from horizontal per ACI 336.3R-14.
  • Pier volume: Volume is computed using a truncated cone (frustum) for the bell plus a cylinder for the shaft above. This is standard engineering practice; contractor billing may use a different method.
  • Minimum reinforcement (ACI 318-19 §26.8.2.1): Applied to shaft gross area only. The bell is mass concrete and is not reinforced per standard practice. Use ρmin ≥ 1.0% for piers in expansive soils; 0.5% otherwise.
  • Minimum bar count: 6 bars for Dshaft ≤ 20 in; 8 bars for Dshaft > 20 in (ACI 336.3R-14 §7.3 / ACI 318-19 §26.8.2.2).
  • Bell uplift resistance: The bell bearing area on top can resist significant uplift. Provide the geotechnical engineer's allowable upward bearing pressure on the bell top, or set to 0 for conservative design.
  • This calculator does not check: structural compression capacity of shaft, lateral/moment capacity, or slenderness. Verify separately if pier is subject to lateral loads or significant moment.
