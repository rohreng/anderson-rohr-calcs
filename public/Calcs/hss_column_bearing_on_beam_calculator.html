3. Base Plate A36 default — bears on beam flange steel, not concrete

Bearing model: uniform pressure over the HSS wall footprint (per AISC 360-22 §K2.1 cap-plate definition — footprint measured across the full HSS width) dispersed at 2.5:1 through the plate thickness to the beam flange. This is a design assumption (DG24 §7.4 cap-plate analogy applied in reverse), not a Specification-prescribed capacity. No rigid-plate/full-N option is offered.

4. Eccentricities defaults = 0

ew does not redistribute plate contact pressure — it drives the top-flange local-bending and DG9 flange lateral-bending/torsion checks, and the transverse patch position feeds the §J10.5 alignment check. It is not subject to the kern limit. ep shifts contact pressure along the bearing patch (length lb); hard validation limit ep ≤ kern = lb/6 (full-contact linear redistribution only) — exceeding this is a blocking error.

5. HSS-to-Base-Plate Weld §J2.4, E70XX default

Demand = Pu,column distributed around the HSS wall perimeter at the plate top face (load enters the plate through the walls and disperses at ~45° through the plate thickness before reaching the 2.5:1 bearing-dispersion footprint used elsewhere); peak-to-average factor from ep is conservatively applied to the weld demand.

Engineering Notes:
  • The composite slab does nothing for this load path — the column force goes straight into the steel top flange. Composite action, per-segment stud accounting, and the concentrated-load stud provision are checked in Calc 3 (composite_stud_blockout_calculator.html), not here.
  • Bearing pressure model: uniform pressure over the HSS wall footprint (measured across the full HSS width, per §K2.1's cap-plate definition of lb), dispersed at 2.5:1 through the base-plate thickness — a stated design assumption (DG24 §7.4 analogy in reverse), not a code-prescribed capacity.
  • Top-flange local-bending check is a novel yield-line/plate-strip model derived and independently bounded in docs/steel-node/calc1-top-flange-spec.md — not a Specification-prescribed capacity.
  • ew-driven torsion/flange-lateral-bending check is a simplified DG9-based screening model (concentrated torque converted to an equal/opposite flange lateral force couple, checked as weak-axis flange bending over Lb) — not a full DG9 torsional analysis. See "Deviations" in the build report.
  • If web local yielding/crippling/compression-buckling fail, stiffeners are the fix and are sized PRELIMINARILY here — coordinate with the brace/gusset geometry in Calc 2 before detailing (they will likely want to occupy the same location).
  • Beam global flexure/shear rows are a spot check against RISA-entered demands only, not a full Chapter F/G design.
  • Web local yielding (§J10.2) and web local crippling (§J10.3) each apply their own Specification end-distance threshold independently (d for J10.2, d/2 with the lb/d branch for J10.3) — no shared "interior if x > d" shortcut.
