Engineering Notes:
  • This is a one-way beam-strip model of the plate (I = b·t³/12 through the thickness). It does not capture 2-way plate action, Poisson effects, or partial edge restraint — treat results as an approximation for a plate whose width b is not small compared to its span L.
  • Weak-axis flexure assumes bending about the plate's minor axis; lateral-torsional buckling does not apply (consistent with AISC 360-22 §F11 for weak-axis bending of rectangular bars/plates) — yielding always governs.
  • Strength check uses the factored (LRFD) demand; deflection check uses the service-level demand. Enter both from your own analysis — this calculator does not compute demand or apply load factors.
  • Uniform Load case assumes bearing pressure is constant across the full top surface of the plate, support to tip. If your actual bearing pressure is non-uniform along the projection, use Point Load with the true resultant location instead — recognize that an equivalent point load reproduces the exact support moment but only approximates true deflection when the real distribution isn't a point load.
  • No shear, bearing, weld, or connection checks are included.
