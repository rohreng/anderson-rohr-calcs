■ 3. Load Case 1 — Gravity / Down Top row tension · bottom edge bearing

Enter ASD service-level loads. For gravity/down, the beam reaction V acts downward at standoff e, putting the TOP anchor row in couple tension and bearing at the BOTTOM plate edge.
Total vertical reaction delivered to the plate (all 4 anchors).
Uniform tension/pull-out shared by all 4 anchors (P/4 each), added to couple tension. P applied at the plate centroid (concentric) — no applied moment.

■ 4. Load Case 2 — Wind / Seismic Reversal Bottom row tension · top edge bearing

Enter ASD service-level loads for the reversed case. The reversed reaction puts the BOTTOM anchor row in couple tension and bearing at the TOP plate edge. Do NOT pre-reduce wind by 0.6 here — use the ⅓ increase checkbox instead.
Total reversed reaction delivered to the plate (all 4 anchors).
Uniform tension/pull-out shared by all 4 anchors (P/4 each). P applied at the plate centroid (concentric).
