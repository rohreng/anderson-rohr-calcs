HSS Hanger — Tension Connection at Beam Bottom Flange

HSS tension member hangs from a plate welded to the underside of a supporting W-shape beam's bottom flange

AISC 360-22LRFD J2.4 WeldsD2/D3 Tension Members J4 Connecting ElementsTable D3.1 Shear Lag J10.1 Flange Local BendingJ10.2 Web Local Yielding
Load path: Tu pulls straight down on the HSS → two longitudinal fillet welds transfer load into the plate → the plate is welded to the underside of the beam's bottom flange → the beam flange (and, informationally, the web) resists the concentrated tensile pull-through. This tool assumes the HSS is slotted over the plate (plate passes through slots cut in the two HSS walls parallel to the load line), consistent with AISC 360-22 Table D3.1 Case 5 (single concentric gusset through a slotted HSS). If your detail instead butts the HSS end against one plate face, the HSS shear-lag check does not apply as modeled — treat that case separately.

Engineering Notes:
  • Geometry assumption: the HSS is modeled as slotted over a single concentric plate (AISC 360-22 Table D3.1 Case 5). If the HSS instead butts against one face of the plate, the HSS wall shear-lag/rupture check is not applicable as computed — treat as a lap weld to a plate face and check the HSS wall in bearing/tearout separately.
  • Beam flange local bending (§J10.1) assumes the connection engages the flange near its centerline (over the web). If the plate/weld only reaches the flange tips (as a hand sketch may show), the force is delivered further from the web than the J10.1 model assumes — this is more conservative than J10.1 predicts, and a yield-line check of the flange overhang should be considered by the engineer of record.
  • Web local yielding (§J10.2) is written for concentrated compressive forces; it is reported here as an informational screening check only for the tensile force delivered into the web through the flange-to-web fillet. Engineering judgment required — not a pass/fail limit state per the letter of the spec.
  • If the connection eccentricity input above is nonzero, the beam is subject to torsion from the offset load — this calculator flags it but does not design for it.
  • Beam flexure/shear under the added concentrated load, and overall HSS member design (L/r, KL/r, etc.), are by others.
  • This is a design aid — results must be reviewed and stamped by a licensed structural engineer.
