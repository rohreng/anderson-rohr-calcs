ELEVATION — Channel Beam Span
Eccentricity e: For a C-channel, the shear center (SC) lies outside the section on the web face side. E₀ = distance from web centerline to SC (DG9 Eq. 3.19). The torque arm is e = E₀ − tw/2 (web outer face to SC). Pv (vertical brace component) applied at the web face creates torque T = Pv × e about the beam’s longitudinal axis. In the cross-section view (looking along beam axis), Pv appears as a vertical force.

4. Eccentricity & Torsion — Out-of-Plane Bending Key Check

Sign convention: E₀ is computed via DG9 Eq. 3.19 (web centerline to SC). The torque arm is e = E₀ − tw/2, from the web outer face to SC. For all standard C-shapes, e > 0 and torsion is always active when Pv ≠ 0.

5. Transverse Stiffener at Brace Connection Optional

A full-depth stiffener plate at the brace point provides a warping-pin (Mw = 0) at that section. For PP end conditions this eliminates warping — σw and τw go to zero. Only St. Venant torsional shear remains: τt = T·t/J. The stiffener carries force couple Fs = T / hfl.

Engineering Notes:
  • Eccentricity: E₀ (DG9 Eq. 3.19) = distance from web centerline to shear center. The torque arm is e = E₀ − tw/2 (from web outer face to SC). For all standard C-shapes, SC lies outside the channel on the web face side, so e > 0 always. Pv is the vertical brace component; in the cross-section view it appears as a vertical force offset from the SC by distance e.
  • Torsion without stiffener: DG9 exact analytical solution (Appendix C). Warping normal stress σw = E·Wno·|θ″|max acts at flange tips and adds to bending and axial stress.
  • Stiffener effect: A full-depth plate at the brace point provides a warping-pin (Mw = 0) at that section. With PP end conditions, this eliminates σw and τw entirely. The stiffener carries force couple Fs = T/hfl. Size the weld to the web for Fs (not included here).
  • FF, FP, and cantilever torsional BC use conservative approximations; PP uses the exact DG9 App. C solution.
