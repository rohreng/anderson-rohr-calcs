Engineering notes & assumptions
  • Section properties are generated from the SSMA standard geometry of each designation by the AISI linear (mid-line) method; capacities use the AISI S100-16 Effective Width Method. Validated against published 600S162-54 values to ~1–2%. Confirm governing members against manufacturer / AISI tables for final design.
  • Header tracks resist out-of-plane wind bending (M = wL²/8), with shear, web-slenderness, combined bending+shear and lateral deflection. Header studs span the opening web-vertical to carry gravity and are checked for combined bending+compression and gravity deflection.
  • Jamb studs use the explicit jamb length and weak-axis brace spacing you enter for axial compression (flexural & torsional-flexural buckling) and the AISI C5.2 combined bending+axial interaction with moment amplification.
  • Distortional buckling (S100 C3.1.4 / C4.2) and web crippling at bearings/reactions are not included — check separately where lips, intermediate bracing, or bearing are marginal.
  • Demands are entered directly from your analysis (ASD level). The wind/seismic factor is applied to flexural demand only. Ωb=1.67, Ωv=1.60, Ωc=1.80.
