Load path: out-of-plane wind spans the opening as bending in the two tracks. Gravity is carried across the opening by the header studs; their end reaction R = wG·b/2 is auto-delivered as axial into each jamb, which also resists its own wind tributary (combined bending + axial).

Engineering notes & assumptions
  • ASD throughout. Demand = applied moment M = wL²/8, shear V = wL/2, axial P. Capacities are allowable: Ma=Mn/Ωb (Ωb=1.67), Va=Vn/Ωv (Ωv=1.60), Pa=Pn/Ωc (Ωc=1.80). D/C = demand / allowable.
  • Jamb axial = auto header reaction R = wG·b/2 (each end of the gravity header) + the additional axial you enter, divided over the jamb studs.
  • Section properties are generated from each SSMA designation's geometry by the AISI linear (mid-line) method; flexural/compression strengths use the AISI S100-16 Effective Width Method (B4 edge-stiffener + B2.3 web with neutral-axis iteration). Validated against AISI D100-17E Design Manual examples: I-1 gross properties (~1%), I-8A effective section Se=1.89 (match), III-1A effective area Ae=0.515 / Fn=37.25 ksi (match). Confirm governing members against manufacturer tables for final design.
  • Distortional buckling (S100 C3.1.4 / C4.2) and web crippling at bearings are not included. AISI Example III-1A shows distortional buckling can govern deep / narrow-flange sections (Ma dropped 62→53 k-in there) — check it separately where lips or intermediate bracing are marginal.
